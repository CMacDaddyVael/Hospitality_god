# STR Platform Changes & Regulations (March 2026)

## Airbnb

### Fee Structure (Now Fully in Effect)
- Transitioned to **15.5% host-only service fee** for all hosts
- Guests no longer see separate Airbnb service fee at checkout
- Massive jump from old 3% host share under split-fee model
- To maintain same net revenue, hosts must mark up nightly rates by ~**18.34%**
- Brazil exception: 16%

### Algorithm Shifts (2026)
- **AI-driven matching replaces traditional search** — Airbnb now functions like a matching engine, not a marketplace. Visibility depends on structured, clean listing data the AI can act on.
- **"Vitality" scoring is the new ranking signal** — calendar updates, response speed, wishlist saves contribute micro-bonuses. Stale listings get buried.
- **New listing boost is GONE** — no more temporary top placement for new listings
- **Content quality weighted like YouTube** — photo quality, title strength, CTR directly determine page-one placement
- **Price is NOT the primary ranking lever anymore** — algorithm rewards reliability, guest satisfaction, conversion rate, consistency over low pricing

## Vrbo / Expedia Group

### Premier Host Program Overhaul (Jan 1, 2026)
- Now evaluated **per listing, not per account**
- Stricter thresholds:
  - 99%+ booking acceptance rate
  - **Zero** owner-initiated cancellations (no tolerance)
  - Minimum 5 reviews with 4.6+ average rating
  - At least 5 bookings or 60 booked nights

### New Features
- **Performance Milestones** — tiered system, hosts unlock search visibility boosts and trust badges (including "Top 1%" designation)
- **Expanded distribution** — Vrbo listings now surface across Expedia ecosystem: 70,000 businesses, 160,000 travel agents, brand partners (Delta, Alaska Airlines, Revolut)
- **New discount tools** — early-bird, last-minute, members-only discounts directly from PMS

## Booking.com

- **Pre-authorized payments** now available for property managers using Payments by Booking.com
- **$1M damage insurance** per reservation + existing $1M liability coverage
- **Personalization-driven discovery** — listings that clearly communicate who they're for and what experience they offer are algorithmically favored

## Regulatory Changes (March 2026)

### Idaho (HB 583 — Passed March 10, 2026)
- Limits what cities/counties can impose on STRs
- Local governments can require safety measures (smoke detectors) but CANNOT require owners to live on-property, hire professional management, or purchase additional insurance
- Wipes out existing rules in McCall and other resort towns

### Washington, DC (Short-Term Rental Regulation Amendment Act of 2026)
- Allows **renters** to list primary residences as STRs (if lease permits, not rent-stabilized)
- New **"special event" license** — residents can rent during designated events/holidays without being present

### European Union (EUSTRR — Takes Effect May 2026)
- **Harmonized registration and platform data-sharing** across all 27 member states
- Most comprehensive STR regulation to date
- Affects any host listing in the EU

### Riverside County, California
- 45-day moratorium on new STR permits in Thousand Palms and B Bar H Ranch (March 3 – April 17, 2026)

## Booking Behavior Shifts (Spring 2026)

- **Shorter booking windows** — especially Gen Z/Millennials booking closer to check-in
- **Shorter stays dominating** — 1-4 night trips outpacing extended vacations (urban + event markets)
- **Selectivity over volume** — guests choosing based on trust, ease, value, cultural alignment, not just price
- **Economic bifurcation** — high-income travelers spending freely; lower-income pulling back due to inflation
- **Market outlook** — cautious optimism, stability or modest growth in ADR/demand/revenue/occupancy
